Aubameyang, Ferran and Adama:


Barcelona's new-look front three for the derby.


Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang in action for Barcelona against Atletico Madrid.FRANCESC ADELANTADO ES LUIS F. ROJO


Ahead of Sunday's derby away to Espanyol, Barcelona head coach Xavi Hernandez is considering going with a new-look front three comprised of recent signings Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang, Ferran Torres and Adama Traore. The Catalan coach is certainly not short of options in attack and one his biggest dilemmas between now and the end of the season will be picking his trident of choice for each match, and perhaps to even settle on a first choice for the biggest games and to enter next season with.

Without counting the injured Ansu Fati, Xavi has 10 players vying for those three jerseys every week: Memphis Depay, Ousmane Dembele, Luuk de Jong, Ferran Torres, Aubameyang, Adama, Abde Ezzalzouli, Ferran Jutgla and Gavi. For the derby on Sunday, the idea is to combine the three new signings from the start for the first time. Against Atletico Madrid last Sunday, Aubameyang began on the bench due to his lack of game time, with Gavi joining Adama and Ferran Torres in attack. Aubameyang is available to start against Los Periquitos, however, while Memphis will not be back in time, but Xavi does hope to have the Dutchman for the Napoli game four days later.
